Responsibilities:

- Provide basic care to residents in long-term care facilities, including bathing, dressing, grooming, feeding, and toileting
- Monitor residents' physical and emotional well-being, reporting any changes or concerns to nursing staff
- Assist with transferring residents, using proper lifting techniques to prevent injury
- Maintain a clean and organized living environment for residents, adhering to facility standards
- Participate in resident care planning, contributing to individualized care plans as needed
- Collaborate with other healthcare professionals to ensure comprehensive care

Required Skills:

- High school diploma or equivalent required; CNA certification preferred
- Basic life support (BLS) certification required
- Ability to lift 50 pounds and stand for long periods
- Strong communication and interpersonal skills
- Compassion, empathy, and ability to work with diverse populations

Preferred Skills:

- Experience working in long-term care facilities or healthcare settings
- Knowledge of medical terminology and basic first aid
- Familiarity with electronic medical records (EMRs) systems
- Ability to speak multiple languages
- Certification in gerontology or a related field
