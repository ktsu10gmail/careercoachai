This proposed architecture makes perfect sense and aligns well with the "architecturally sound framework" described in the sources for a SaaS-based recruitment platform
. Your plan effectively separates the Employer Experience (Company Setup & Management) from the Applicant Experience (Discovery & Submission), which is essential for a professional B2B product
.
Here is how your vision integrates with the existing system logic and database structure:
1. Employer Entry Point: recruit-system.jetta.com
This serves as your primary B2B landing page. As you suggested, this page is exclusively for employers (HR and IT Admins) to sign in or sign up
.
Company Onboarding: During signup, the system creates a record in a (logical) Companies table and allows the HR Admin to input Company Principles and Ethics
.
Backoffice Access: Once signed in, the HR Admin accesses their dashboard to copy-paste Job Descriptions and configure the 70/30 weighted scoring logic
.
2. The Company Job Board: recruit-system.jetta.com/companyname
This acts as a dynamic aggregator for all active jobs linked to that specific company.
Dynamic Listing: The system queries the Jobs Table for all job_id entries associated with that company_id that have an "Active" status
.
Distribution Hub: The employer can share this main URL on their corporate "Careers" page, or they can take individual Unique Job URLs generated by the system to post on external job boards like LinkedIn or Indeed
.
3. Applicant Entry Point: The Unique Job URL
The sources define the Applicant UI as a "Gateway" that is triggered only when a candidate interacts with a specific job-linked doorway
.
Contextual Sign-In: When an applicant clicks a link from your /companyname board or an external site, they are routed to a page specific to that job_id
.
The "Single Session" Workflow: At this point, the applicant is prompted to sign in or sign up. Following the system's "minimum effort" design, they then immediately proceed to upload their resume and answer the standardized 20 questions in a single session
.
Why This Structure Works for Your SaaS Model
Privacy and Security: By keeping the employer setup on a separate root domain and providing company-specific sub-paths, you reinforce the "Privacy by Design" architecture where applicant data is siloed per company
.
Reduced Friction: This flow supports the "Pre-Screening Accelerator" concept by ensuring applicants only see the interface when they are ready to apply for a specific role, preventing them from getting lost in a general employer-facing site
.
Scalability: This URL structure allows you to host "many companies" on the same platform while giving each one a branded, dedicated space (/companyname) for their talent pool
.
Suggested Database Update
To support the /companyname URL, you would simply add a company_slug or company_name field to your Companies Table, ensuring it is unique across the platform. The Jobs Table would then use a company_id (Foreign Key) to ensure that the correct jobs appear on the correct company-specific front page
.
